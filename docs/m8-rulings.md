the goalpost-moving the pre-registration discipline exists to prevent. If
we want the general question answered, it gets its own pre-registered
gate in a future milestone.

## R14 — Upgrade economy (ask 3): STANDS AS-IS. The residual is re-classified, not filled.

**User ruling:** the rest-vs-smith decision is a core StS skill axis —
"if you are good, you get ahead of the power curve (upgrade instead of
rest); if you are not, you don't." The 11–14 pt residual between natural
uptake and the dose-controlled ceiling is **skill-expression headroom,
not a design gap.** Our rest policy models a cautious pilot; the dose
cells model perfect access; the distance between them is where getting
good lives, and filling it would flatten the game's central campfire
decision.

Consequences, all binding:
- **All upgrade-access content options are CLOSED for this purpose**
  (post-elite rewards, cheaper/heal-hybrid smithing, egg/Whetstone
  analogues as fixes). Relic designs may still exist as ordinary content
  on their own merits, but nothing ships with "closing the residual" as
  its justification. Talent Training remains v2 shelf space under its
  original (Burst-upgrade) motivation only.
- **For the DECISIONS log: dose-controlled/oracle cells are diagnostics,
  never acceptance targets.** Natural-world numbers under the standing
  baseline are the law. Nothing may cite a dose-cell winrate as what an
  archetype "should" reach.
- Optional, low priority, purely informational (fits "pilot/policy
  suspect first"): a greedy-smith rest-policy variant would measure how
  much of the residual good piloting actually recovers — a skill-curve
  characterization, not a balance input. Whenever convenient, or never.
